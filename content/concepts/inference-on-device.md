## Summary
This thesis argues that always-on agentic usage, latency sensitivity, privacy, and falling hardware costs create a strong case for inference shifting partly from centralized clouds toward local devices and edge endpoints.

## Core claims
- Persistent everyday agent use may favor on-device inference because cloud costs compound with constant interaction volume.
- Latency-sensitive consumer workflows become more useful when inference happens near or on the endpoint.
- Hardware improvements can make edge inference practical across a wider range of devices over time.

## Main tension
- The strongest bear case in the memo is memory: local devices may not have enough RAM or bandwidth to support the model sizes and context lengths users will want.
- As a result, the likely outcome may be hybrid inference rather than full cloud replacement.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Extends the agentic stack from cloud infrastructure toward endpoint deployment economics.
- Sits downstream from [[nvidia]]'s model and hardware roadmap, but can also reduce dependence on centralized inference for some tasks.
- Complements [[agentic-utilities]] by shifting part of the agentic workload from core cloud to edge and device surfaces.
