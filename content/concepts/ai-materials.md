## Summary
This theme argues that AI demand should increasingly accrue to upstream material suppliers rather than only to obvious chip and server winners. As compute, packaging, optics, and power systems scale, the hidden bottlenecks can sit in specialty materials and process inputs.

## Core claims
- Material intensity rises as AI systems move toward denser packaging, wider-bandgap power, and more advanced optical interconnects.
- Investors may underweight suppliers whose exposure is obscured by weak legacy end markets or by their distance from the headline AI trade.
- The most durable upside often sits where supply is specialized, qualification cycles are long, and substitution is difficult.
