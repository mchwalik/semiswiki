## Summary
This theme argues that naval and maritime industrial capacity is becoming strategically important again as defense priorities broaden beyond software and missiles. The setup favors companies tied to shipbuilding, maintenance, and related supply chains if policy support keeps building.

## Notable expressions
- GD US, HII US, TKMS GR

## Citrindex basket constituents
- The Citrindex shipbuilding sleeve is broader than the initial summary implied, including Graham, Kongsberg Maritime, ESCO Technologies, Samsung Heavy Industries, HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, BWX Technologies, Wartsila, Hanwha Ocean, [[thyssenkrupp-marine-systems]], Rolls-Royce, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, General Dynamics, Huntington Ingalls, Hensoldt, Furuno Electric, Conrad Industries, Austal, Chugoku Marine Paints, and Fincantieri.
- This makes the theme look less like a pure naval-prime bet and more like a maritime industrial-capacity basket spanning yards, propulsion, sensors, components, naval suppliers, and defense-adjacent OEMs.
