## Summary
This is the parent layer for trades whose main driver is regime change rather than product adoption. It groups ideas where valuation depends primarily on elections, central-bank divergence, sanctions, macro easing, or national policy normalization.

## Why this second-layer bucket exists
- Several pages in the wiki are really about policy path dependency, but they previously sat beside consumer and AI concepts with no explicit distinction.
- This bucket helps separate regime-sensitive setups from structural technology or behavioral-demand theses.

## Boundary rules
- Use this bucket when the main re-rating mechanism is electoral change, monetary policy, sanctions, regulation, or national strategic posture.
- Use [[strategic-industrial-capacity]] when the thesis is instead about physical scarcity of yards, materials, transport, or production capacity.
- Use [[consumer-wallet-and-attention]] when the trade is mainly about household-level behavior rather than top-down policy.
