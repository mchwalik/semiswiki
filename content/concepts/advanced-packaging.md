## Summary
This thesis treats advanced packaging as a durable AI bottleneck rather than a commodity backend step. As model sizes, HBM demand, and interconnect density rise, packaging architecture becomes central to performance, power, and yield.

## Core claims
- Packaging complexity is increasing alongside AI accelerator scale, making substrate, bonding, and integration capacity more valuable.
- The memo presents architecture-level constraints as a reason packaging should capture more of the industry profit pool.
- Intel is highlighted as a potentially underappreciated beneficiary because of its packaging footprint and strategic relevance.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Connects directly to [[photonics-cpo-supply-chain]], where hybrid bonding, silicon photonics integration, and CPO all depend on advanced packaging workflows.
- Reinforces [[ai-power-supply-chain-inheritance]] because denser compute and higher rack power increase the value of better package-level efficiency.
- Ties back to [[nvidia]] as the main system roadmap forcing the packaging stack to evolve.

## Citrindex basket constituents
- The Citrindex dump shows a substrate- and PCB-heavy sleeve featuring Glotech Industrial, Unimicron, Nan Ya Printed Circuit Board, Austria Technologie & Systemtechnik, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Elite Material, Zhen Ding, Ibiden, TTM Technologies, Gold Circuit, ASE Technology, Sanmina, Celestica, and [[amkor]].
- It also shows a narrower packaging sleeve centered on Intel, [[amkor]], Kulicke & Soffa, [[synopsys-company]], and BE Semiconductor Industries.
- Together these constituents make the thesis much more concrete: Citrini is expressing advanced packaging through substrate makers, outsourced manufacturing, packaging equipment, and a few strategic system-level enablers rather than through GPU designers alone.
