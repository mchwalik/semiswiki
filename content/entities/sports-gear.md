## Overview
Sports Gear is highlighted as a Taiwanese cleated-footwear manufacturer with contract exposure to major global brands, making it a less obvious but operationally direct way to express incremental World Cup-related soccer demand.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Acts as a manufacturing-side expression of [[world-cup]].
- Complements [[adidas]] by representing the supply-chain layer behind branded soccer footwear.
- Adds a less obvious Asia-listed component to a mostly consumer-facing basket.

## Key angles
- The appeal lies in contract manufacturing leverage to major brands rather than consumer marketing.
- It is a more indirect but potentially cleaner way to capture soccer-footwear volume growth.
- The thesis depends on brand demand translating into production orders rather than only retail sell-through headlines.
