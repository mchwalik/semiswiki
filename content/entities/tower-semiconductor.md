## Overview
Tower Semiconductor is a silicon-photonics foundry beneficiary of the AI optics buildout, especially when indium-phosphide laser scarcity and EML tightness accelerate the shift toward SiPho-based architectures.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Connects [[photonics-cpo-supply-chain]] to foundry capacity rather than only to lasers, tools, and transceiver vendors.
- The interview strengthens the case by arguing that EML shortages and CW-laser reallocation make silicon photonics more urgent, directly benefiting Tower.
- Provides a practical bridge between the InP bottleneck and downstream CPO deployment described in [[rating-ai-supply-chain-bottlenecks]].

## Key angles
- The interview says customers are effectively paying Tower up front for future capacity, signaling unusually tight SiPho demand.
- Earlier source material already framed Tower as investing heavily to expand silicon-photonics shipments.
- Risk is that capacity expansion or architecture changes ease the scarcity premium faster than expected.
