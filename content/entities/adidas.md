## Overview
Adidas appears as a straightforward global soccer-equipment and apparel beneficiary of the World Cup, positioned to capture both immediate tournament spending and a longer secular rise in US soccer interest.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Functions as a global branded-sports expression within [[world-cup]].
- Links the US-hosted tournament to worldwide soccer merchandising and footwear demand.
- Pairs naturally with [[sports-gear]] as the branded and contract-manufacturing sides of the same demand chain.

## Key angles
- The thesis combines event-driven volume with longer-term soccer adoption in the US.
- Adidas matters because it is one of the most direct listed ways to express soccer demand rather than general travel demand.
- It adds an international branded-goods layer to a trade otherwise dominated by travel and lodging names.
