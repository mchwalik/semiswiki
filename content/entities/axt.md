## Overview
AXT is presented as part of the substrate and materials layer of the indium-phosphide supply chain, making it an upstream way to express the laser bottleneck behind CPO and next-generation AI optics.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Adds a raw-materials and substrate angle to [[photonics-cpo-supply-chain]] beyond the more visible laser names and tool vendors.
- Supports the argument in [[rating-ai-supply-chain-bottlenecks]] that the real scarcity is not mined indium but the specialized crystal, wafer, and processing chain.
- Sits naturally beside [[iqe]] and [[sumitomo-electric]] as part of the less obvious InP bottleneck layer.

## Key angles
- The interview explicitly lists AXT among the substrate-world names that remain bottlenecked.
- This makes the company a way to express upstream scarcity rather than only end-demand for transceivers.
- Risk is that substrate exposure is harder for the market to underwrite than laser-capacity names with more visible AI demand.
