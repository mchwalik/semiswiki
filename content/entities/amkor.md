## Overview
Amkor is presented as a beneficiary of lower-value but still technically advanced packaging work that may be pushed outward as Intel and TSMC reserve premium internal capacity for their highest-priority AI programs.

## Relevance in this wiki
- One of the clearest entity expressions inside [[advanced-packaging]].
- Adds outsourced packaging exposure to a wiki that already covered the concept but not the company layer.
- Represents the practical spillover effect of in-house capacity constraints at leading fabs.

## Key angles
- The thesis is structural: crown-jewel programs can force valuable overflow into outsourced channels.
- Amkor benefits not from owning the highest prestige node, but from absorbing rising advanced-volume demand.
- Risk is that leading-edge customers keep more packaging in-house for longer than expected.
