## Overview
Airbnb appears in the World Cup trade as a flexible lodging beneficiary when hotel capacity tightens and host-city demand spills into surrounding neighborhoods, creating a more event-sensitive upside than the market may fully price.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Acts as a direct lodging and alternative-accommodation expression of [[world-cup]].
- Benefits when major event demand pushes travelers beyond traditional hotel inventory, complementing [[choice-hotels-international]].
- Helps the wiki capture platform-style travel beneficiaries inside the broader [[26-trades-for-2026]] basket.

## Key angles
- Longer event-related stays can magnify Airbnb’s exposure relative to one-night hotel demand.
- Local restrictions matter, but overflow into suburbs and secondary areas can still support bookings.
- The thesis is incremental rather than company-defining, so upside depends on the event acting as a demand kicker.
