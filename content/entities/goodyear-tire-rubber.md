## Overview
Goodyear is cited as evidence that EV and autonomy trends can lift tire revenue and margins, making it a practical single-name anchor for the idea that replacement and premium-tire demand rise with heavier, more intensely used vehicles.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Provides a named company bridge for [[kicking-the-tires]].
- Connects fleet-intensity and autonomous-mobility themes back to [[next-gen-autonomous]].
- Shows how a seemingly old-economy supplier can benefit from modern vehicle-technology transitions.

## Key angles
- EV tire fitments can carry higher revenue and better margins than ICE equivalents.
- Robotaxi and high-mileage fleet usage increase replacement cadence even if overall vehicle maintenance falls.
- The trade works best if investors continue to underappreciate tires as a durable autonomy pick-and-shovel.
