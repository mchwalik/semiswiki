## Overview
Enel is presented as a primary European-utility beneficiary if gas and power-market conditions normalize, with lower volatility and better spark spreads improving earnings visibility and balance-sheet flexibility.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Is one of the named outperformers in [[long-european-utilities]].
- Links regional energy-market normalization back to the macro setup in [[dovish-fed-hawkish-ecb]].
- Provides a concrete company expression for a trade that otherwise reads as an abstract power-market thesis.

## Key angles
- Lower TTF volatility reduces collateral and balance-sheet stress left over from the 2022 energy shock.
- The source highlights Enel’s debt expansion during the crisis, making normalization especially meaningful.
- Upside depends on falling input costs and policy conditions improving faster than investors expect.
