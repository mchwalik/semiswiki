## Overview
Choice Hotels is framed as a down-market lodging beneficiary of World Cup overflow demand, with the memo arguing that even secondary hotel brands can benefit when premium inventory is constrained across host markets.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Represents the traditional-hotel side of [[world-cup]].
- Complements [[airbnb]] by capturing overflow demand that cannot be absorbed by higher-end or sold-out lodging options.
- Extends the wiki’s event-driven trade coverage into more modestly positioned travel companies.

## Key angles
- The thesis depends on scarcity rather than luxury positioning.
- Choice benefits specifically because mega-events push travelers toward whatever inventory is available.
- This is more of a situational occupancy and pricing-power trade than a structural hospitality re-rating.
