## Overview
Dick’s Sporting Goods is framed as an obvious but still underappreciated beneficiary of World Cup-related demand, with the memo arguing that prior concerns around tariffs, firearms controversy, and store-footprint worries have faded enough for the company to absorb a soccer-driven spending bump.

## Relevance in this wiki
- Represents one of the clearest US retail expressions inside [[world-cup]].
- Pairs with [[academy-sports-outdoors]] as a sporting-goods beneficiary of rising soccer demand.
- Shows the trade can include established incumbents, not only niche or sponsorship names.

## Key angles
- The memo treats Dick’s as more attractive because old overhangs have eased.
- Demand upside comes from both the event itself and longer-term soccer adoption.
- The setup is stronger as a retail absorber of category demand than as a direct World Cup sponsor story.
